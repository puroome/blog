Mason: 911. What’s the emergency?


Avery: There’s had been an earthquake here. An abandoned warehouse collapsed.


Mason:  What’s your location?


Avery: I’m in Anaheim, at Lincoln Avenue and State College.


Mason: Is anyone injured?


Avery: Yes, one man is hurt.


Mason: Is he unconscious?


Avery: No, he’s alert, but he’s bleeding.


Mason: We are sending an ambulance now. Please stay on the line.


Avery: Okay…

The following are useful phrases you can use when there is a disaster.

Practice saying each statement. Think of scenarios where you can use

them. You may also add more examples on the list.




• There’s a wildfire.


• Run!


• I can’t breathe.


• I can’t see because of the smoke.


• It’s unbelievable.


• Surveyors are assessing the damage done to buildings.


• It's a catastrophe.


• Quick! Hide under the desk!


• We can only give them our moral support.


• Where’s the fire extinguisher?


• It will take us a few months to come to terms with what's happened.


• International organizations are providing emergency relief.


• We hope the government will learn the lessons from the earthquake and spend more money on

research.


• Monitoring will help us be better prepared in the future.


• Please find my brother.


• A typhoon is coming.
