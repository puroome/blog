Weather (날씨): The state of the atmosphere at a particular place and time.

​

Forecast (예보): A prediction or estimate of future weather conditions.

​

Temperature (온도): A measure of the degree of heat or coldness of the air.

​

Humidity (습도): The amount of moisture present in the air.

​

Precipitation (강수): Any form of water that falls from the atmosphere to the Earth's surface, such as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

​

Rain (비): Water droplets that fall from the atmosphere in the form of liquid.

​

Snow (눈): Ice crystals that fall from the atmosphere in the form of white flakes.

​

Cloud (구름): A visible mass of condensed water vapor floating in the atmosphere.

​

Wind (바람): The natural movement of the air, usually indicated by its direction and speed.

​

Sunny (맑음): When the sky is clear and filled with sunshine.

​

Cloudy (흐림): When the sky is covered with clouds.

​

Partly cloudy (구름이 조금 있는): When the sky has a mixture of clouds and clear areas.

​

Overcast (흐린): When the sky is completely covered with clouds.

​

Fog (안개): A thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the air near the Earth's surface, affecting visibility.

​

Thunderstorm (천둥번개): A weather phenomenon characterized by thunder, lightning, heavy rain, and sometimes strong winds.

​

Hurricane (허리케인): A tropical cyclone with strong winds rotating around a central low-pressure area.

​

Tornado (토네이도): A violent, rotating column of air in contact with both the surface of the Earth and a cumulonimbus cloud.

​

UV Index (자외선 지수): A measure of the strength of the ultraviolet radiation from the sun.

​

Visibility (시야): The distance at which objects can be clearly seen in the atmosphere.

​

Barometer (기압계): An instrument used to measure atmospheric pressure.

일기예보에서 자주 사용되는 영어 회화 표현을 정리해드리겠습니다. 다음은 일기예보 관련 표현들입니다:

​

What's the weather like today?

(오늘 날씨는 어떤가요?)

​

It's going to be sunny.

(맑을 것 같아요.)

​

We can expect some rain later.

(나중에 약간의 비가 올 것으로 예상됩니다.)

​

There will be scattered showers throughout the day.

(하루 종일 소나기가 있을 것입니다.)

​

The temperature will reach a high of 25 degrees Celsius.

(온도가 25도까지 오르겠습니다.)

​

It will be mostly cloudy with a chance of thunderstorms.

(대부분 흐리고, 천둥번개가 있을 수 있습니다.)

​

Make sure to bring an umbrella.

(우산을 가져가는 것을 잊지 마세요.)

​

Dress warmly, as it's going to be chilly.

(추울 것이니 따뜻하게 옷을 입으세요.)

​

The winds will be gusty, reaching speeds of up to 40 miles per hour.

(바람이 강하게 불며, 최대 속도로 40마일에 이를 것입니다.)

​

Due to heavy snowfall, there may be travel disruptions.

(폭설로 인해 여행에 지장이 있을 수 있습니다.)

The UV index is high today, so remember to wear sunscreen.

(오늘 자외선 지수가 높으니 자외선 차단제를 바르는 것을 잊지 마세요.)

​

The fog will cause limited visibility on the roads.

(안개 때문에 도로의 시야가 제한될 것입니다.)

​

We are under a tornado watch. Please take necessary precautions.

(우리는 토네이도 주의보 아래에 있습니다. 필요한 예방 조치를 취하세요.)

​

The weather conditions are expected to improve by tomorrow.

(날씨 상황은 내일부터 좋아질 것으로 예상됩니다.)

​

Stay updated with the latest weather forecasts.

(최신 날씨 예보를 확인하세요.)

​

이러한 표현들은 일기예보 관련 영어 회화에 자주 사용되는 표현들입니다.

필요에 따라 문맥에 맞게 사용하면 도움이 될 것입니다.

​

​

A: Hey, have you checked the weather forecast for tomorrow?

(내일의 일기예보를 확인했니?)

​

B: No, I haven't. What does it say?

(아니, 아직 안 봤어. 어떻게 되어 있어?)

​

A: It's going to be partly cloudy with a high of 28 degrees Celsius.

(구름이 조금 끼고, 최고 기온은 섭씨 28도가 될 거야.)

​

B: Sounds pleasant! Is there any chance of rain?

(좋아 보이네! 비 올 확률 있어?)

​

A: Yes, there's a 30% chance of scattered showers in the afternoon.

(네, 오후에는 소나기가 30% 확률로 있을 거야.)

​

B: Alright. I'll make sure to carry an umbrella, just in case.

(알겠어. 혹시나를 대비해서 우산을 가져갈게.)

​

A: Good idea. Oh, and be aware that there might be gusty winds reaching speeds of up to 35 miles per hour.

(좋은 생각이야. 그리고 바람이 강하게 불어서 최대 35마일의 속도로 달릴 수도 있으니 주의해.)

​

B: Thanks for letting me know. I'll dress accordingly and be cautious outdoors.

(알려줘서 고마워. 옷차림도 그에 맞게 하고, 밖에서 조심하겠어.)

​

A: Also, the UV index is quite high tomorrow, so don't forget to apply sunscreen.

(그리고 내일 자외선 지수가 꽤 높으니 자외선 차단제 바르는 거 잊지 마.)

​

B: I'll keep that in mind. Thanks for the heads-up!

(알겠어. 미리 알려줘서 고마워!)

​

A: You're welcome! Stay updated with the latest forecast for any changes. Have a great day tomorrow!

(천만에요! 변동 사항이 있을 경우 최신 예보를 확인하고, 내일 좋은 하루 보내세요!)

​

B: Thank you! You too!

(고마워요! 당신도 좋은 하루 보내세요!)
