Crime 범지관련 영어회화 및 유용한표현


 



Ryan: 911. How may I help you?


Eli: I need to report a break-in. It happened last night.


Ryan: Where did the break-in happen?


Eli: It happened at my house.


Ryan: Was anything taken?


Eli: I'm not sure yet.


Ryan: What makes you think there was a break-in?


Eli: My front window was busted.


Ryan: I'll send a squad car.


Eli: Please hurry






Crime 범지관련  유용한표현


Here are sample phrases and sentences you can use when reporting to

the police or talking about crime.


• Help me. I lost my luggage.


• Last night, four stores were broken into.


• A man tried to rob our bank today.


• I was in the bank during the robbery.


• Did you get a good look at his face?


• The suspects were wearing a mask.

• Do you know why I pulled you over?


• I had nothing to do with the crime that was committed.


• A bunch of people went looting last night.


• The police don't know who the suspect is.


• I'm sure the police will catch him eventually.


• The suspect took the money and tried to get away.


• I pulled you over because your brake light is out.


• I would like to plead not guilty.
