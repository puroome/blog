Victor: What’s that?

Tanya: It’s a list of questions I’m going to ask at the childcare center when we visit

today. I want to be prepared.


Victor: We already know that it’s licensed and has a good reputation. What other

questions are you going to ask?


Tanya: Lots. For instance, I want to know what the ratio is of children to each

childcare worker and what their staff turnover rate is, their policies on

disciplining the kids and what kinds of meals and snacks they serve.


Victor: Those are all good questions.


Tanya: I want to find out their policies on sick kids and if they allow late pick-up.


Victor: Yeah, I guess those are really good questions, too.





Day Care Center 탁아소  유용한 표현



The following are relevant expressions and questions you can use when

inquiring at a day care center. Can you add more expressions on the list?



• How long have you been running a daycare?


• Are you licensed?


• What hours are you available?


• What if I am late picking up or dropping off?


• How much do you charge?


• Do you have some references?


• Are you certified in child CPR?


• What is the ratio of children to teacher?


• How do you discipline a child?


• Do you have the children nap?


• How many and what are the ages of


the children you're currently looking after?


• Are the children separated by age group?


• What is your curriculum?


• I’d like to leave my child here.


• When does the morning class begin?


• Thank you for taking care of my son.


• Does he get along well with other children?


• He always forgets his school supplies.


• Do you give snacks?
