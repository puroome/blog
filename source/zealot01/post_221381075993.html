게시일: 2018. 10. 7.

Elon Musk is ranked 25th on Forbes list of most POWERFUL people in the World.
And for good reason!
SpaceX and Tesla are amongst the most innovative and exciting companies in the world!

But great leaders can only get so far if they don’t have talented people to support their cause.
So, for this video, we are going to zero in on Musk’s two most valuable employees who are legends in their own right.
The first is Jeff or J.B. Straubel, the Chief Technical Officer of Tesla.
Struabel was born in Wisconsin and he had a passion for learning how things worked at a very early age.
He would often find things around him to take apart and put back together and he even constructed a chemistry lab in his parent’s basement.
When he was 13 he found an old golf cart in the dump and he brought it home, rebuilt the electric motor and restored the cart to working condition!
Anyway, Straubel went on to Stanford where he earned his Masters in Energy Engineering in 2000.
Straubel and his friends from Stanford were one of the first people to consider using lithium-ion batteries to power vehicles as the technology was rapidly improving.
After college, he bought an old Porsche for $1600 and converted it into an electric car.
In order to do so, he had to build a charger from scratch and write software to make it work.
And that car ended up setting the World Record for EV acceleration, traveling a quarter mile in 17.28 seconds.
During this time, he was trying to find support in making fully electric vehicles and he met Elon Musk in 2003 who loved the idea and gave Straubel $10,000 towards the efforts.
Later, Musk set up a meeting between Straubel and Tesla’s Founders Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning and Struabel was hired on the Tesla team in 2004 and the rest is history.
In 2008, Straubel was named one of the top 35 innovators under the age of 35 in the World by MIT Technology Review.
Today, Straubel is considered one the World’s Foremost Battery Experts and as the CTO of Tesla, Straubel oversees the technical direction and engineering design including battery technology, power electronics, motors, software, firmware, and controls.

Now let’s move on to Tom Mueller.
Mueller was born in Idaho and like Straubel he was grew up obsessed with tinkering with things around him such as clocks and lawn mowers.
And it wasn’t long until he turned his attention to rockets.
He started out with mail-ordered rocket kits and then at 12 years old, he started making his own Rockets from scratch.
He used his dad’s welding equipment to construct a Rocket Engine Prototype for a school project which would go on to win several regional science fairs.
Mueller went on to receive his Masters in Mechanical Engineering at Loyola Marymount University in 1992 and eventually landed a job at the Aerospace and Automotive company TRW Inc where he would work for 15 years.
While at TRW, He worked as a lead engineer during the development of the TR-106 rocket engine which was one of the most powerful engines ever at the time it was constructed.
During that time, he joined the Recreation Research Society which was a group of amateur rocketeers and Mueller would build rocket engines as a hobby in his spare time.
He eventually fabricated a 36 kg rocket engine capable of producing 57 kN of thrust! Incredible!
And that engine became The World’s largest liquid-fueled rocket engine built by an amateur!
Then in early 2002, Mueller met Musk, and the two hit it off right and Musk brought Mueller on the SpaceX team shortly after where he his first job was to design and build the famous Merlin Rocket Engine Family which propels the Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy Rockets and holds the highest thrust-to-weight-ratio record.
The Merlin is a world-class rocket engine and what makes it so great is that it is very easy and low-cost to make and extremely reliable.
But for the past four years, Mueller has been working on something FAR BIGGER AND MORE EXCITING.
He’s been designing the Raptor Engine, which is supposed to be twice as powerful than the Merlin engine and have an even better thrust-to-weight ratio!
And more excitingly, it will propel the BFR, the Space Launch Vehicle that will take humanity to MARS!
Today, Mueller is hands down one the World’s foremost Spacecraft propulsion experts.

Now let’s bring it back to where we started, Musk has led many talented engineers between SpaceX and Tesla, but it’s clear that his most valuable are Straubel and Mueller, both world-renowned experts in their own fields.
Musk, Straubel, and Mueller all have one striking similarity.
Musk taught himself computer programming at 12 years old, Straubel restored golf carts at 13 and Mueller built rockets at 12.

니콜라 테슬라(1856~1943)는 발병가이자 전기 및 기계공학자, 물리학자이다.

그는 19세기 말~20세기 중반에 이르기까지 가장 뛰어난 과학자 중의 한 사람이며,

다양한 이론과 발명품을 남겼다.

최초의 교류 유도 전동기와 테슬라 변압기 등을 발명했고 특허는 272개에 달한다.


오늘날 우리가 사용하는

교류전기 시스템과 고주파 변압기, 유도 전동기, 네온 사인, 교류 발전기, 집적회로, 모터, 라디오 등

수많은 발명품을 남긴 장본인이다.



[테슬라가 남긴 말]

"전력(電力)이라는 것은 어디에나 무제한의 양으로 존재한다.

세상의 기계 장치들을 움직일 수 있다."



그의 연구영역은 당시로서는 믿을 수 없었던

무선에너지 전송 및 무선통신 기술, 기상조절, 레이더, 전자 광선 무기, 에테르 연료 자동차,

비용이 들지 않는 무한공간에너지의 활용, 다른 행성과의 교신기술에 이르기까지 광범위했었다.


그는 1899년 미국 콜로라도에 있는 동안

자신이 다른 행성의 지성체(지적 생명체)로부터 신호를 받았다고 대담하게 언급한 바가 있으며,

1931년 <타임 매거진>과의 인터뷰에서 다음과 같은 의미심장한 언급을 했다.


"나는 행성 간의 교신보다 더 중요한 것은 없다고 생각한다.

그것은 언젠가 이루어질 것이며, 우주 내에 우리처럼 활동하고 고민하고 노력하는

다른 인간들이 존재한다는 확신은 인류에게 마법적인 결과를 낳을 것이다."


그는 백년 전인 그 때 이미 전선줄 없이 전기 에너지를 송전하는 방법과

프리 에너지 활용법을 알고 있었다.

즉, 지구 자체가 이미 엄청난 전기의 힘을 지니고 있으므로 뽑아 쓰기만 하면 아무런 비용도 들이지 않고

무제한으로 전기에너지를 쓸 수 있다는 것을 증명하고 구현하려고 했던 것이다.
